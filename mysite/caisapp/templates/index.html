Understanding the procedure of Closed Captioning quality assessment by Deaf or Hard of Hearing viewers

Study Description

In this study, we are investigating the quality assessment of Closed Captioning (CC) for four different factors from a viewer point of view.
The four different factors include:

  1. Delay between caption appearance and audio (or visual);
  2. Speed of caption presentation;
  3. Number of missing words in a caption; and
  4. Paraphrasing factor: i.e., where the captions are not verbatim.

This study has three parts:

  1. Pre-study demographic questionnaire section
  2. Caption quality rating section:

    You will be presented with a series of short video clips.
    The videos will have A.I. predicting human satisfaction ratings for each of the four quality factors mentioned above.
    After watching each clip with captions,

    1. If you agree with the predictions, you can proceed to the next video.
    2. If you disagree with the predictions, teach your satisfaction ratings.

    After each submission, the A.I. will try learning from your input
    (even if you agree with the prediction, it will still learn from the consensus).
    Please wait (~ 20s) while the A.I. is learning from your input.

  3. Semi-structured interview (via Zoom/Google Meet):
    You will be asked about four questions relating to the general experience of TV live Closed Captioning service
    as well as your experience with the quality rating session in this study.
