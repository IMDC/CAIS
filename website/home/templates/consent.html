Understanding the procedure of Closed Captioning quality assessment by viewers who are Deaf or Hard of Hearing

Study description

In this study, we are investigating the quality assessment of Closed Captioning (CC) for four different factors from a viewer point of view. The four different factors include:

  1. Delay between caption appearance and audio (or visual);
  2. Speed of caption presentation;
  3. Number of missing words in a caption; and
  4. Paraphrasing factor: i.e., where the captions are not verbatim.
This study has three parts:

  1. Pre-study demographic questionnaire section
  2. Caption quality rating section:

    You will be presented with a series of video clips from four genres (weather forecasts, sports, sports-talk shows, and breakfast talk shows). The videos will have A.I. predicting human satisfaction ratings for each of the four quality factors mentioned above. Then, you will watch short 20 ~ 30 sec video clips and decide whether you agree/disagree with the predicted ratings.

    • If you agree with the predicted ratings,
      • you can proceed to request the next video.
    • If you disagree with the predicted ratings,
      • please report your satisfaction ratings for each of the caption quality factor.

    After each submission, the A.I. will try learning from your input (even if you agree with the prediction, it will still learn from the consensus). Please wait (~ 20s) while the A.I. is learning from your input.

  3. Semi-structured interview (via Zoom/Google Meet):
    You will be asked about four questions relating to the general experience of TV live Closed Captioning service as well as your experience with the quality rating session in this study.

At mutually agreed upon meeting time, there will be a virtual meeting room where you will start the study. The investigator should provide you a URL where you can link to begin the study. The website will present the pre-study demographic questionnaire followed by the video rating session. Because of the nature in this study, the entire session can be monitored by screenshare, and we may request to record your screen. You may ask any questions to the investigator throughout the process via either text chat or a video conversation. The semi-structured interview will be conducted after you finished videos using either text chat or a video conversation. If you require an ASL interpreter, please notify the investigator 2-weeks in advance than the scheduled date.

How long will the study take to complete?

Maximum 90 min.

Risks and Discomforts

The risks associated with the study are minimal. You may experience some fatigue while watching video clips. You may take breaks during the session if you become fatigued. You may also discontinue the study at any time without penalty. You may also experience some discomfort in answering some of the questions in the questionnaire. You are not required to answer any question that makes you uncomfortable. The data collected will be used to analyze the subjective quality assessment in CC and to develop a method of evaluation reflecting this assessment. Only members of the research team will have access to all the data, which will be stored on password-protected project computer at the IMDC. Read the Privacy Statement

Data Dissemination

  • The collected data will only be disseminated in the form of aggregated and summarized result (no raw data will be available to public).
  • Once data are collected and subjected to analysis, it will no longer be available to withdraw your data.
  • If you wish to have your data withdrawn from this study, please make a request to do so before leaving the study.
  • Once you leave the study, your data could be included in the final analysis.

Confidentiality and Data Storage

  • All data will be confidential and will not be published except as summary data.
  • All data will be used exclusively for research and educational purposes.
  • Anonymous data not associated with yourself in any way will be made public through the release of the aggregated findings of this study.
  • Data will be kept on a password-protected server at the Inclusive Design and Media Centre (IMDC), a locked at Ryerson University.
  • Only the principal investigator, Somang Nam, and his supervisors, Prof. Mark Chignell and Prof. Deborah Fels, will have access to this data.
  • The data will be stored for five years and then destroyed.
  • In the case of transportation, the data will be shared via Ryerson Google Shared Drive, which is secure and encrypted.
  • If you provide your First and Last name or email address, your survey responses may no longer be anonymous to the researcher. However, no names or identifying information would be included in any publications or presentations based on these data, and your responses to this survey will remain confidential.

Expected Benefits

Though participants will not directly benefit from their participation in this study, the data will be used to develop a new method of measuring the quality of CC and potentially improve the quality of CC. Final copies of findings of the study can be requested from Somang Nam somang[at]mie.utoronto.ca once such findings are complete. Participants wishing to receive a copy of this report directly can leave their email address on this consent form.

Voluntary Nature of Participation

Participation in this study is voluntary. Your choice of whether or not to participate will not influence your future relations with Ryerson University/the University of Toronto. If you decide to participate, you are free to withdraw your consent and to stop your participation at any time without penalty or loss of benefits to which you are allowed. At any point in the study, you may refuse to answer any question or stop participation altogether.
